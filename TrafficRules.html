Important Traffic Rules

Wearing Seat Belt

Under Section 138 (3) CMVR 177 MVA, not fastening a seat belt while driving is a traffic rule violation and can invite a fine and penalty from the authorities.

Follow Traffic Signals

Drivers need to follow the traffic signal light. Violation of this can invite a challan from the authorities. Repeated traffic rule violations can also lead to suspension of a driving licence.

Avoid Drinking & Driving

Drivers need to follow the traffic signal light. Violation of this can invite a challan from the authorities. Repeated traffic rule violations can also lead to suspension of a driving licence.

Avoid Driving Without Insurance

Driving a car without valid motor insurance is a punishable offence and can invite a fine from the authorities.

Avoid Electronic Device While Driving

The new law MV rules that came into effect on 1 October 2020 state that the driver can use mobile phones for navigational purposes while driving, but using them for talking or texting is a violation of traffic rules.

Avoid Racing & Speeding

Further, at high speeds, maintaining control of your vehicle is difficult. Hence, to curb overspending, authorities have decided the speed limit to follow on the road. Violation of the speed limit leads to a penalty.

Do not Overload the Car

Another crucial car driving rule is not overloading a car with passengers. A car that carries passengers beyond its capacity can throw off the vehicle's balance and handling.

Driving Without Valid Documents

The vehicle's registration certificate (RC) must be carried at all times to prove that the car is registered with the relevant authorities.

Follow Parking Regulations

Always park in designated areas, and avoid parking in spaces reserved for specific purposes, such as disabled parking or loading zones.

Offer Way to Emergency Vehicles

If you are on a multi-lane road, safely move to the left or right, creating a clear path for the emergency vehicle to pass through the centre.
